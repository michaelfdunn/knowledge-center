# Research Gaps: K. Admin & Consumer Personas

> These personas cannot be reliably written from the available research files.
> Do not use synthesized versions of these personas in design decisions without dedicated research.

---

## K. Admin (System-Level) — ⚠️ THIN DATA

### The gap
No participant in the uploaded files was studied explicitly as a **system-level knowledge admin** — someone who configures the KM platform globally, manages plugins, controls user criteria, and owns KB-level settings across the instance. The iLab participants included people with admin-adjacent roles (Knowledge Domain Engineers, Knowledge Process Owners, Platform Owners), but none in a dedicated system admin capacity.

### What can be attributed without assumption
- Calvin (Knowledge Specialist) noted that "User Criteria" is inaccessible to him — implying admins have elevated access that authors do not.
- Natalya distinguishes the admin role from the KM role: admins own the config widget and perform one-time KB setup; KMs do ongoing governance.
- The Merge Duplicates study (project knowledge) noted that de-dup capability conflicts with article versioning and this feature is **disabled** — a decision that lives with admins, not authors or KMs.
- AMAZE plugin users (EAE Challenges) struggle to upgrade without ServiceNow support, which is an admin-scoped problem.
- From the Health Score Executive Summary (project knowledge): admins want configurable health score weights and governance-based insights by ownership group.

### What's needed to close this gap
Interview notes from the Merge Duplicates study (Nvidia, Caterpillar, Wells Fargo, Vanguard participants — identified in project knowledge as platform owners and knowledge admins) are the closest available source. These exist in project knowledge in summarized form but are not quote-level accessible from the uploaded files. Pulling those interview notes would substantially improve fidelity.

## Consumer (End User / Article Reader) — 🚫 MISSING

### The gap
The Consumer — the person who reads knowledge articles to resolve issues, answer questions, or complete tasks — **does not appear as a research participant in any of the uploaded files.** The iLab studies tested the **Knowledge Center management interface** with knowledge managers, authors, and specialists. No consumer-facing research is present.

### What the research says about consumers indirectly
KMs speak about consumers constantly, but as an audience they're trying to serve — not as voices in the data.

- Top drivers of an effective KB (Knowledge Health Score Executive Summary): clarity and plain language (35%), up-to-date articles (28%), effective search (28%), well-structured content (14%), accuracy and trustworthiness (14%). These are reported by KMs based on what they observe consumers needing — not by consumers directly.
- KMs want metrics on consumer behavior — time on page, ticket deflection, whether the article resolved the issue — because they have no direct window into the consumer experience.

The only direct consumer-adjacent voice in the full project knowledge set:

> *"I want to see how often users successfully solve their issue without escalating to support, what they search for but don't find, and where they drop off."*
> — Software Developer (as consumer), UMGC (Health Score survey, project knowledge)

One quote. One person. Not sufficient for a persona.

### What's needed to close this gap
Usability studies or interviews with agents or employees who use the knowledge portal to find answers. This is a research population entirely absent from all uploaded files and would require a new study to address.
