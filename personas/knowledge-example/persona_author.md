# Persona: Calvin — Knowledge Author / Specialist

## Who they are
Calvin is a Knowledge Specialist at ServiceNow's SURF team — a dedicated article author who manages his own articles and uses the Knowledge Center as a home base for his personal workload. He is not managing a whole KB, but he owns specific articles and expects the platform to center on them. He came into the iLab expecting a personal insights hub with action items; he found a hub that showed him everyone else's data alongside his.

## Their job in one sentence
Success is getting through his queue of articles that need updating, knowing what requires attention today, and publishing well-formatted content without fighting the editor.

## Their relationship to knowledge / the platform
Regular, task-oriented use. He works in the article editor, manages the lifecycle of his articles, and uses the Knowledge Center homepage as a dashboard for his personal workload. Intermediate expertise — knows the platform well enough to notice when it's wrong, not just when it breaks.

## What they care about most
Scoping: seeing *his* articles, not the whole company's. Clear urgency signals: knowing which of his articles actually need action right now. An editor that doesn't require workarounds. Expiration windows that create genuine urgency — not 90 days, which feels too far away to motivate action.

## Their biggest frustration right now
The platform's "your articles" view doesn't actually show his articles.

> *"Currently it does not populate my personal articles. Rather, it populates a list of articles for various owners."*
> — Calvin Canchola, Knowledge Specialist, ServiceNow SURF

> *"The 'Your articles' section does not bring me to a page that is filtered for my KBs. It brings me to the list of all KBs."*
> — Calvin Canchola (Round 2)

> *"I think the requires attention section should be at the top of the page."*
> — Calvin Canchola

On the authoring side (from EAE Challenges research): copying content from Word or SharePoint breaks formatting. No table of contents support. Image placement is limited. Preview doesn't reliably match published output:

> *"I've had issues where the preview looked good but not when published and had to go back and forth editing and publishing until it looked good."*
> — EAE Discovery Research participant

## How they talk
Practical and direct. Uses "my articles," "my KBs," "ownership group," "action items." Gives specific, actionable suggestions. Not technical but not vague — describes problems in terms of what the system showed vs. what he expected. Casual but businesslike.

## Research grounding
- **Primary:** iLab survey (Raw_Data_iLab.xlsx) — Calvin Canchola, ServiceNow SURF, "Knowledge Specialist," both rounds.
- **Authoring context:** EAE Challenges sheet (EAE_Challenges_Needs_and_Jobs.xlsx) — Challenges 1–4 describe authoring pain points directly applicable to this persona: formatting, image management, template reuse, copy-paste from external tools.
- **Jobs framing:** EAE Needs & Jobs sheet — Jobs 1–7 describe what the Author needs from an editor to create engaging, well-organized, reusable content efficiently.

## Red flags that make them disengage
A "Requires Attention" section that surfaces every article in the system, not his. A "Your Articles" link that navigates to an unfiltered list. An editor that requires knowing HTML workarounds (e.g., using a table to add image padding) to achieve basic formatting.

## The question they always ask that designers hate
*"Is this showing me my stuff, or everyone's stuff?"*
