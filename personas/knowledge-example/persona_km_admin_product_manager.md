# Persona: Natalya — Knowledge Admin / KM Product Manager

## Who they are
Natalya is a product-side knowledge manager — she owns the KM platform itself as much as any individual KB. At ServiceNow's SURF team her title is Product Manager for SURF Knowledge. She thinks in systems: workflows, governance, persona-based configuration, feature rollout. She is the most strategically fluent participant in this research, and her feedback reads less like user complaints and more like design briefs.

## Their job in one sentence
Success is a Knowledge Center that different personas can actually use effectively, with workflows that surface the right work to the right person and configuration that scales without hand-holding.

## Their relationship to knowledge / the platform
High-frequency, high-depth. Uses all features and evaluates them structurally. Tests the platform and reads results critically. She is also the person in an organization who would set up features for others — she thinks from both her own perspective and that of her downstream users.

## What they care about most
Persona-aware design — she explicitly calls out that the config widget is for admins, the management widgets are for KMs, and these are different people who should not be looking at the same interface. Actionability: widgets should surface insights that drive action, not just display data. Personalization and flexibility: she envisions a Knowledge Center configurable like a browser homepage.

## Their biggest frustration right now
Features exist without clear ownership or intent. The labeling is generic in ways that obscure what things actually do.

> *"I'm not sure if 'Knowledge features' resonates for all items listed. It's more like an action landing page to manage your knowledge: articles, feedback, workflows, features, groups, etc. related to your breadth of Knowledge Bases and persona. Right now it seems the term 'features' is a catch all for all the cool KM work."*
> — Natalya Lemchuk, Product Manager – SURF Knowledge

> *"Actions to me is more 'Requires Attention' as opposed to what's currently in the Actions widget. That widget is comprised of 'Create' and 'Configuration of Groups.' Maybe consider having separate widgets for Create, Optimize, and Consume."*
> — Natalya Lemchuk

Featured articles appear blank or stale because they're manually curated with no fallback. The 90-day expiration threshold feels too coarse; she wants 30 days as the action threshold.

## How they talk
Strategic and constructive. Uses terms like "persona," "call to action," "widget config," "governance," "breadth of Knowledge Bases." Positive but pushes hard on specifics. Would say *"awesome! I know more features are coming and can't wait to dive in"* — immediately followed by four concrete suggestions. Never vague. Always has a "what I would envision" follow-up.

## Research grounding
- **Primary:** iLab survey (Raw_Data_iLab.xlsx) — Natalya Lemchuk, ServiceNow SURF, "Product Manager – SURF Knowledge." Responded across both rounds with the most detailed open-text feedback of any participant.
- **Context:** Knowledge Health Score Executive Summary and HealthScore Design Feedback — pattern of KM/admin wanting consolidated, actionable, role-scoped views.

## Red flags that make them disengage
Generic labels that don't differentiate by persona. A "catch-all" design that makes no distinctions between what an admin needs vs. what a manager needs vs. what an author needs. Inconsistent data parameters — she asked: *"what date range is this data from?"* Blank states with no explanation for why they're blank.

## The question they always ask that designers hate
*"Which persona is this designed for, specifically?"*
