# Persona: Theresa — Knowledge Manager

## Who they are
Theresa manages one or more knowledge bases for a large organization. She did not create most of the articles she manages and inherits ownership of a sprawling content ecosystem — 4,000+ articles — that she has varying degrees of control over. She is not primarily a writer; she is a steward, a traffic cop, and a quality enforcer. She works daily in the ServiceNow backend, not the consumer portal.

## Their job in one sentence
Success is a knowledge base where the right people can find accurate, up-to-date content, feedback tasks are actioned before they pile up, and authors are doing their part without her having to chase them.

## Their relationship to knowledge / the platform
Daily interaction, primarily in the backend admin interface. Manages KBs, workflow approvals, article reviews, and feedback triage — often across dozens of authors she doesn't directly supervise. Experienced enough to know exactly what's broken and why.

## What they care about most
Getting a clean, scoped view of *her* KB — not the entire company's. Knowing which articles need action and who is responsible. Being able to reach authors when their articles are expiring or have open feedback tasks, without relying on emails no one reads. Metrics tied to actual outcomes, not just view counts.

## Their biggest frustration right now
The platform shows her everything rather than what's relevant to her role and her KB.

> *"Expiring articles — shows all articles, not ones that have anything to do with me or my work. It is about 8,400 results which are not meaningful to me."*
> — Theresa Daudier, Knowledge Base Manager, Now Support

> *"My articles — shows articles I revised or updated, which is not helpful as a KB manager because I revise and update many articles that are not 'mine'. (4.2K results!)"*
> — Theresa Daudier, Now Support

> *"Configurations should be set up by knowledge base. We're not allowed to change global settings, so it's pointless to show settings that would apply across all knowledge bases."*
> — Theresa Daudier, Now Support

The analytics dashboard she most wants to use doesn't load. She wants a one-click way to notify authors through Teams when their articles need action — because "emails are ignored and no one looks at the messaging feature in the platform."

## How they talk
Precise, efficient, institutional. Uses terms like "knowledge base," "expiry date," "feedback tasks," "ownership group," "workflow state," "retired articles." Not fluffy — says what she wants and what's broken without softening it. Moderately formal in feedback; gets blunter the more broken something is. Would never say "nice experience" without immediately qualifying it.

## Research grounding
- **Primary:** iLab survey (Raw_Data_iLab.xlsx) — Theresa Daudier, ServiceNow Now Support, "Knowledge Mgmt" role. Responded across both Round 1 and Round 2 with detailed open-text critique on nearly every section.
- **Corroborating:** Multiple Now Support and CVS Health participants in similar roles (Jenna Palacio, Jeanne Wilkens, Marjorie Reynolds, Daniel Maldonado) showed overlapping frustrations with unscoped data and broken navigation.
- **Context:** Knowledge Health Score Executive Summary — KM leaders want metrics tied to outcomes, not system stats. "Insights are not actionable."

## Red flags that make them disengage
Showing data that isn't scoped to her KB or role. Features that are visible but not accessible (locked configs, dashboards that don't load). Terminology she doesn't recognize — she flagged: *"'Featured articles' — what does this mean? How are these selected? What makes an article 'most useful'? Those two labels are not clear."* If the platform makes her feel like she's looking at someone else's work, she stops trusting it entirely.

## The question they always ask that designers hate
*"Does this only show articles from my knowledge base, or is it mixing in everything?"*
