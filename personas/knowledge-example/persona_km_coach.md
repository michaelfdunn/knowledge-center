# Persona: Jeanne — KM Coach / Process Lead

## Who they are
Jeanne works in Knowledge Management at Now Support and functions as a process lead and informal KM coach — she's the person who guides others through the platform, troubleshoots workflow edge cases, and advocates for usability improvements upward. She has institutional depth. She tested the Knowledge Center and the article editor and came with specific expectations about how both should work, grounded in how she would explain them to others.

## Their job in one sentence
Success is a platform where KM workflows run smoothly enough that she's not the one manually explaining every step to every new author who gets lost.

## Their relationship to knowledge / the platform
High-frequency, holistic, experienced. She moves through authoring, approvals, configurations, and resources in a single session. She notices inconsistency in UI patterns and flags them precisely. She is the person who would design and run KM onboarding training — and she evaluates the platform through that lens.

## What they care about most
Process consistency and teachability. Clear, logical workflows — especially in article creation, where navigation across left nav, right nav, and center panel creates confusion. SSO for linked resources so she doesn't have to re-authenticate mid-workflow. Knowing which approvals are pending and which are time-sensitive.

## Their biggest frustration right now
The article editor's navigation is non-linear in a way that's hard to explain and harder to learn.

> *"Instead of staying in the center and writing down, you start in the left navigation to add information, then move to the right navigation to grab the template form, back to the left to edit the title field that was just overwritten, and finally to the center to add the content. I couldn't find the title — I had to do a CTRL-F to find it."*
> — Jeanne Wilkens, Knowledge Management, Now Support

> *"It would be nice if it were SSO — if I am logged in, I don't have to log into another page once I click on it."*
> — Jeanne Wilkens

She also flags that NowAssist is configured for a limited set of sources that don't match the KBs her team actually uses, limiting its utility for her org.

## How they talk
Enthusiastic but exacting. Gives structured, numbered feedback. Uses terms like "left nav," "right nav," "approval," "SSO," "template form," "NowAssist." Comfortable with technical language but communicates it plainly. Warmly constructive — would say "Looks great!!" and mean it, then immediately produce four numbered improvements.

## Research grounding
- **Primary:** iLab survey (Raw_Data_iLab.xlsx) — Jeanne Wilkens, ServiceNow Now Support, "Knowledge Management," both rounds. Produced some of the most detailed authoring and configuration feedback in the dataset.

## Red flags that make them disengage
A UI that doesn't follow the workflow it's supposed to support. Resources that require a separate login. Features presented without descriptions — she flagged inconsistency in Knowledge Features: *"I would have all or none under the titles"* (some items had descriptions, others didn't). Anything she couldn't explain to a new user in a straight line.

## The question they always ask that designers hate
*"If I'm training someone new on this, what do I tell them the order of steps is?"*
